Eligibility

Who Needs an ITIN?

If you're a foreign national with a US tax obligation but no Social Security Number, an ITIN is your required tax identifier. Here are the most common situations:

🌎

Foreign Nationals with US Income

Earning wages, rental income, or investment returns from US sources requires a tax ID for IRS filings.

👫

Spouses & Dependents

Non-US spouses or children of US taxpayers who need to be listed on a joint federal tax return.

📈

Investors & Property Owners

Foreign investors with US real estate, brokerage accounts, or partnership interests subject to US tax.

📜

Treaty Benefit Claimants

Non-residents claiming reduced withholding under a US tax treaty must have an ITIN on file.

Questions

Frequently Asked


What is an ITIN and who needs one?
An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a nine-digit number issued by the IRS to individuals who have a US tax filing or reporting requirement but are not eligible for a Social Security Number. This includes foreign nationals earning US income, non-resident spouses of US taxpayers, and foreign investors with US holdings.
Do I need to be physically present in the US to apply?
No. Our service is fully remote. You can complete the entire process — intake, document upload, and e-signature — from anywhere in the world. The only physical component is our certified mail submission to the IRS, which we handle on your behalf.
What if my ITIN expired? Can you renew it?
Yes. ITINs not used on a federal tax return for three consecutive years expire. We handle ITIN renewals through the same W-7 process. When you begin the application, indicate that you are renewing an existing ITIN and provide your prior ITIN number.
How long does the IRS take to issue an ITIN?
The IRS typically processes W-7 applications within 7–11 weeks of receiving them. During peak filing season (January–April), processing can take slightly longer. We monitor your application timeline and notify you when your ITIN is issued.
Is my payment refundable if I'm found ineligible?
Yes. If our eligibility screener determines you do not qualify before payment, you owe nothing. If you've paid and our review team determines you are ineligible after document review, we will issue a full refund. We will never file an application we believe will be rejected.
Can I send original documents or do you need copies?
You do not need to send original documents through our service. We work with certified copies or high-resolution color scans. Original documents sent directly to the IRS can take months to be returned — our process avoids this risk entirely.
What happens after I submit my application?
You'll receive a Case ID and access to your personal client portal. From there, you can track your application stage in real time — from document review through IRS filing to ITIN issuance. We send automated email updates at every milestone.
